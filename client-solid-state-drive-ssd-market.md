<p><p>The Client Solid-State Drive (SSD) market features various NAND flash memory types, primarily MLC, TLC, and 3D NAND. MLC (Multi-Level Cell) stores two bits per cell, offering a balance between performance and cost, suitable for consumer applications. TLC (Triple-Level Cell) stores three bits per cell, providing higher density and lower costs, making it popular for mainstream use. 3D NAND technology enhances storage density and performance by stacking memory cells vertically, increasing reliability and efficiency in increasingly demanding client applications.</p></p>
