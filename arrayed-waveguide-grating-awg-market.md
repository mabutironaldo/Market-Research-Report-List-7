<p><p>Arrayed Waveguide Gratings (AWGs) are optical devices used for wavelength multiplexing and demultiplexing in telecommunications and data networks. The market for AWGs is primarily divided into two types: thermal AWGs and athermal AWGs. Thermal AWGs are temperature-sensitive and require stable environmental conditions to maintain performance. In contrast, athermal AWGs are designed to minimize temperature dependence, providing stable operation across various temperatures, making them ideal for diverse applications. Both types support efficient wavelength division multiplexing (WDM) for enhanced data transmission.</p></p>
