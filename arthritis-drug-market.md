<p><p>The arthritis drug market encompasses various treatment types targeting inflammation and pain relief. Synthetic DMARDs (Disease-Modifying Antirheumatic Drugs) slow disease progression, while biological DMARDs use targeted therapies to alter immune responses. NSAIDs (Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs) reduce pain and inflammation, whereas corticosteroids provide rapid relief by suppressing immune activity. Analgesics focus on pain management without addressing inflammation. This diverse range of medications reflects the complexity of arthritis treatment, catering to different patient needs and disease severity.</p></p>
