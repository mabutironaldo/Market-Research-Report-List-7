<p><p>Sourdough markets can be categorized into Group Type and Flake Type. Group Type sourdough refers to starter cultures propagated from a common source, allowing bakers to share and maintain a consistent fermentation process. In contrast, Flake Type sourdough involves dehydrated cultures that are easy to store and transport, offering convenience for bakers. Both market types cater to different needs, with Group Type fostering community among bakers and Flake Type providing practical solutions for consistent flavor and texture in baking.</p></p>
