<p><p>The weed control services market is categorized into two main types: warm-season weeds and cool-season weeds. Warm-season weeds thrive in higher temperatures and are typically prevalent during summer months, requiring targeted control measures like herbicides and pre-emergent treatments. In contrast, cool-season weeds emerge in cooler temperatures, often in spring and fall, necessitating different strategies such as specific selective herbicides. Understanding these distinctions helps providers tailor their services to effectively manage and mitigate weed growth in various climates and conditions.</p></p>
