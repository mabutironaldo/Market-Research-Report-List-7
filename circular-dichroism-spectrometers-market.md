<p><p>Circular dichroism spectrometers utilize different types of light sources to analyze chiral molecules. Linearly polarized light sources provide a uniform light wave orientation, enabling basic measurements of optical activity. Circularly polarized light sources emit light with a specific handedness, enhancing sensitivity and accuracy in detecting chiral properties. Multiple light sources combine both linear and circular polarization, offering versatile applications and greater flexibility in experiments. Each type caters to various research needs in structural biology, chemistry, and material science.</p></p>
