<p><p>The preserved fresh flower market includes various drying methods to maintain floral aesthetics. Air drying utilizes natural air circulation to remove moisture, creating rustic, dried arrangements. Freeze drying preserves petals by quickly freezing and then evaporating moisture in a vacuum, retaining color and shape. Silica gel drying involves immersing flowers in silica gel crystals to absorb moisture, offering vibrant, preserved blooms. Other methods may include chemical preservation or oil infusion, expanding options for consumers seeking lasting floral beauty.</p></p>
